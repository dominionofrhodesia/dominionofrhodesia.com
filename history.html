History of the Dominion of Rhodesia

The Dominion of Rhodesia was a semi-autonomous dominion within the British Empire, established in 1923. It covered the territories of modern-day Zimbabwe and parts of Zambia.

Early History

Before the declaration of the Dominion, the area was known for its rich indigenous cultures and was impacted by European colonization in the late 19th century.

Dominion Status

In 1923, the region was granted dominion status, allowing for a degree of self-governance while still recognizing the authority of the British Crown. This period saw significant economic development, though it was marked by social tensions and disparities.

Later Developments

The Dominion faced various challenges leading up to its eventual dissolution, culminating in the establishment of the Republic of Rhodesia in 1965.
